Preventing Food Waste

Food Waste in the World

One third of the food produced each year (approximately 1.3 billion tonnes) is wasted – enough to prevent hunger worldwide.

Shopping Tips

Go shopping with a list and buy as much as you need. In campaigns like "Buy two, pay for one", choose only the amount you can consume.

Portion Control

Start with small portions and buy more if needed. At restaurants, share larger portions or take out leftovers.

Planned Consumption

Put products that are close to expiration up front. Make a weekly meal plan and use up leftovers.

Proper Storage

Store foods at appropriate temperatures. Check your refrigerator regularly and keep it below 4°C.

Evaluate Excess Foods

Deliver the food you cannot consume to those in need or share it with your neighbors.

Practical Tips

Shopping List

Before every shopping, check your closet and make a list.

Smart Storage

Put old products in front, new ones in back.

Leftover Food

Use leftover food in different recipes.

Food Recycling Guide

Compost Making

You can obtain natural fertilizer for your garden by composting fruit and vegetable waste, coffee grounds and egg shells.

Food Leftovers

You can turn stale bread into breadcrumbs and make soup broth from vegetable peels.

Animal Shelters

You can repurpose unconsumed and unspoiled food by donating it to local animal shelters.

Correct Storage Methods

Fridge Organization

Store greens wrapped in damp paper towels, fruit in separate drawers and mushrooms in paper bags. The ideal temperature is between 1-4°C.

Tips for Vegetables

Store root vegetables such as carrots and celery in moist sand, onions and garlic in a cool, dark place, and potatoes in a place protected from light.

Tips for Fruits

Separate ripe fruits from others. Keep bananas away from other fruits. Store strawberries unwashed and wash them just before consuming.

Dry Foods

Store bread, nuts and grains in airtight containers in a cool, dry place. Avoid storing bread in the refrigerator.
